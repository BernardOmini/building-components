Building Components

Introduction

Building Components are the individual parts that combine to form a complete Building Structure. Each component performs a specific function and contributes to the strength, stability, comfort, and appearance of the building. A good understanding of Building Components is essential for students and professionals in Building Construction, and Civil Engineering because every building depends on these components working together effectively.

Classification of Building Components

Building components are broadly classified into:

1 Substructure

The portion of a building below ground level.

Components of the Substructure

  1. Foundation
  2. Plinth

2 Superstructure

The portion of a building above ground level.

Components of the Superstructure

  1. Walls
  2. Floors
  3. Doors
  4. Windows
  5. Lintels
  6. Column
  7. Beam
  8. Slab
  9. Stairs
  10. Roof
  11. Finishes

Major Building Components

1 Foundation

The foundation is the lowest part of a building and one of its most important components. It transfers the weight of the entire structure safely to the soil beneath it.

A properly designed foundation prevents excessive settlement, cracking, and structural failure. Foundations may be shallow or deep depending on the nature of the soil and the load of the building. Common types include strip foundations, pad foundations, raft foundations, and pile foundations.

Functions of Fundation

  1. Supports the building.
  2. Distributes loads to the soil
  3. Prevents settlement
  4. Provides stability

Types of Foundations

  1. Strip Foundation
  2. Pad Foundation
  3. Raft Foundation
  4. Pile Foundation
  5. Combined Foundation

2 Plinth

The plinth is the part of a building between the ground level and the finished floor level.

Functions of Plinth

  1. Raises the building above ground level
  2. Protects against moisture
  3. Improves appearance

3 Walls

Walls are vertical structural elements used to enclose spaces and support loads. They provide privacy, security, and protection from weather conditions such as wind, rain, and sunlight. Walls can be classified as load-bearing walls or non-load-bearing walls. Common wall materials include bricks, blocks, stone, concrete, timber, and glass.

Functions of Wall

  1. Provide privacy
  2. Support loads (in load-bearing structures)
  3. Protect occupants from weather

Types of Walls

  1. Load Bearing Wall
  2. Non Load Bearing Wall
  3. Partition Wall
  4. Cavity Wall
  5. Retaining Wall
  6. Curtain Wall

4 Floors

Floors are horizontal surfaces that provide usable space within a building. They divide the structure into different levels and support occupants, furniture, and equipment. Floors should be strong, durable, and capable of resisting wear and tear. Common floor finishes include tiles, terrazzo, concrete, timber, and marble.

Functions of Floor

  1. Support occupants and furniture
  2. Transfer loads to walls or beams
  3. Provide a working surface

Types of Floors

  1. Ground Floor
  2. Suspended Floor
  3. Timber Floor
  4. Concrete Floor
  5. Raised Floor

5 Doors

Doors provide access into and out of buildings and rooms. They also offer privacy, security, and protection. Doors are available in different materials such as wood, steel, aluminium, glass, and composite materials. The choice of door depends on its intended use and location.

Functions of Door

  1. Allow Entry and Exit
  2. Provide Security
  3. Ensure Privacy
  4. Enhances Appearance
  5. Reduce Noise
  6. Acts as ans Emergency Exit

Types of Doors

  1. Panel Door
  2. Hinged Door
  3. Flush Door
  4. Sliding Door
  5. Folding Door
  6. Revolving Door
  7. Metal Door

6 Windows

Windows are openings provided in walls to allow natural light, ventilation, and visibility. They improve indoor comfort by allowing fresh air to circulate within the building. Different types of windows include casement windows, sliding windows, louvre windows, and fixed windows.

Functions of Window

  1. Provide ventilation
  2. Allow natural lighting
  3. Improve aesthetics

Types of Windows

  1. Casement Window
  2. Sliding Window
  3. Fixed Window
  4. Louvered Window
  5. Bay Window
  6. Pivoted Window

7 Lintel

A lintel is a horizontal structural member placed above doors, windows, and other openings in a building. Its main function is to carry the load of the wall above the opening and transfer it safely to the side supports (walls or columns). Without lintels, openings in walls would weaken the structure and could lead to cracks or collapse above doors and windows.

Functions of Lintel

  1. Support the wall above Openings
  2. Transfer Loads to Adjacent Walls
  3. Prevent Cracking
  4. Allows Construction Openings

Types of Lintels

  1. Timber Lintel
  2. Stone Lintel
  3. Brick Lintel
  4. Steel Lintel
  5. Reinforced Concrete Lintel

8 Columns

Columns are vertical structural members designed to carry loads from beams, slabs, and roofs and transfer them safely to the foundation. They are usually made of reinforced concrete, steel, or timber. Columns play a vital role in supporting multi-storey buildings and large structures.

Functions of Column

  1. Support Vertical Loads
  2. Transfer Loads to the Foundation
  3. Support Beams and Slabs
  4. Enable Multi-Storey Construction

Types of Columns

  1. Reinforced Concrete Column
  2. Steel Column
  3. Timber Column
  4. Brick Column
  5. Circular Column
  6. Rectangular Column

9 Beams

Beams are horizontal structural members that support loads from slabs, walls, and roofs. They transfer these loads to columns or load-bearing walls. Beams help distribute loads evenly throughout the structure and improve its stability. Reinforced concrete and steel are common materials used for beams.

Functions of Beam

  1. Support Loads
  2. Transfer Loads
  3. Distribute Load Evenly
  4. Maintain Structural Stability
  5. Allow Large Open Spaces

Types of Beams

  1. Simply Supported Beam
  2. Cantilever Beam
  3. Continuous Beam
  4. Reinforced Concrete Beam
  5. Steel Beam

10 Slabs

A slab is a flat horizontal structural component used to form floors and roofs. It is usually made of reinforced concrete and is designed to carry loads safely. Slabs distribute loads to supporting beams and columns and contribute significantly to the strength of a building.

Functions of slab

  1. Seperate Building Levels
  2. Support load
  3. Forms Roofs and Floors
  4. Provides a Floor surface

Types of Slabs

  1. One-Way Slab
  2. Two-Way Slab
  3. Flat Slab
  4. Ribbed Slab
  5. Waffle Slab

11 Stairs

Stairs provide a means of vertical movement between different floors of a building. They consist of components such as steps, risers, treads, landings, handrails, and balusters. Proper stair design ensures safety, convenience, and ease of movement for users.

Functions of Stairs

  1. Increase Building functionality
  2. Connect different parts of a Building
  3. Facilitate movement between levels
  4. Serve as emergency escape routes

Types of Stairs

  1. Straight Stair
  2. DogLegged Stair
  3. Open Well Stair
  4. Spiral Stair
  5. Helical Stair

12 Roof

The roof is the uppermost covering of a building. Its primary function is to protect the structure and its occupants from weather elements such as rain, sunlight, wind, and heat. Roofs may be flat, pitched, hipped, or curved depending on the design requirements. Common roofing materials include aluminium sheets, zinc sheets, tiles, and concrete.

Functions of Roof

  1. Protects from rain, sun, and wind
  2. Enhances the building's appearance
  3. Provides insulation

Types of Roof

  1. Flat Roof
  2. Gable Roof
  3. Hip Roof
  4. Pitched Roof

13 Finishes

Building finishes are materials applied to surfaces to improve appearance, durability, and protection. Examples include plastering, rendering, painting, tiling, and ceiling finishes. Finishes enhance the aesthetic quality of a building while protecting surfaces from damage and weathering.

Functions of Finishes

  1. Improve appearance
  2. Protect surfaces
  3. Increase durability

Types of Finishes

  1. Plastering
  2. Rendering
  3. Painting
  4. Tiling
  5. Cladding

Importance of Building Components

  1. Provide structural strength
  2. Ensure stability and safety
  3. Protect occupants from weather
  4. Improve comfort and functionality
  5. Enhance the appearance of the building
  6. Increase the lifespan of the structure
